In the classic sci-fi film "The Empire Strikes Back," Luke Skywalker travels to the swamp planet of Dagobah to train under the wise Jedi Master Yoda. As the Rebel Alliance faces a brutal defeat on the ice planet Hoth, Luke rushes to Cloud City to save his friends from a trap set by the Galactic Empire. He enters a fierce lightsaber duel with the villainous Darth Vader, during which Luke's hand is severed and Darth Vader reveals the shocking truth that he is actually Luke's biological father, completely altering the fate of the galaxy.

"The Sixth Sense" follows Malcolm Crowe, a dedicated child psychologist in Philadelphia, who tries to help a troubled young boy named Cole Sear. Cole is terrified because he possesses an isolated, supernatural ability to see and communicate with deceased spirits who do not know they are dead. Malcolm works tirelessly to mend the boy's psychological trauma, only to realize at the very end of the film that Malcolm himself was actually dead the entire time, having succumbed to a gunshot wound from the opening scene of the movie.

In Martin Scorsese's psychological thriller "Shutter Island," US Marshal Teddy Daniels and his new partner Chuck Aule travel to a secluded psychiatric facility for the criminally insane to investigate the disappearance of a patient. Teddy faces uncooperative staff, severe migraines, and a massive storm that traps him on the island while he uncovers clues pointing to unethical human experiments. However, the final confrontation reveals that Teddy is not a marshal at all, but is actually Andrew Laeddis, the institution's most dangerous patient who invented the mystery to cope with murdering his wife after she drowned their children.
